People Making A Change

  • Greta Thunberg:
    Greta Thunberg, a young climate activist from Sweden, became a global symbol for climate action. She began by striking from school every Friday to protest government inaction on climate change, sparking the worldwide “Fridays for Future” movement. Greta has spoken at the United Nations, met world leaders, and inspired millions of young people to demand urgent action. Her work shows how a single person with passion and persistence can raise awareness and influence global conversations about climate change.

  • David Attenborough:
    Sir David Attenborough, a British natural historian and broadcaster, has spent decades educating people about the environment. Through his documentaries, he has highlighted the beauty of the natural world and the threats it faces due to climate change and human activities. His storytelling makes complex environmental issues accessible to millions of viewers, encouraging people to take action and protect the planet. Attenborough’s work emphasizes the importance of knowledge and awareness in inspiring change.

  • Wangari Maathai:
    Wangari Maathai, an environmental activist from Kenya, founded the Green Belt Movement, which focused on planting trees to combat deforestation and empower communities. She worked to improve women’s rights while addressing environmental issues, planting millions of trees in Kenya. Maathai became the first African woman to win the Nobel Peace Prize for her efforts, showing how environmental activism can be combined with social change to make a lasting impact

  • Elon Musk:
    Elon Musk, the entrepreneur behind Tesla and SpaceX, has contributed to climate change solutions through technological innovation. Tesla’s electric vehicles and solar energy products aim to reduce reliance on fossil fuels, while his investments in renewable energy and battery technology promote sustainable energy use. Musk’s work demonstrates how innovation, business, and technology can be powerful tools in addressing climate change.

  • Jane Goodall:
    Jane Goodall, a renowned primatologist and environmentalist, has dedicated her life to wildlife conservation and environmental protection. Through her research and advocacy, she has raised awareness about the importance of forests, ecosystems, and sustainable living. Her Roots & Shoots program encourages young people worldwide to take action in their communities, showing that individual efforts can grow into global movements.
